FAQ

1. Why does the URL have Kirby-Bauer in it?

This simulation is based on the Kirby-Bauer disc-diffusion antibiotic sensitivity test. The test is used to determine the susceptibility of bacteria to various antibiotics.

2. How do I use this simulation?

Select and add as many antibiotic disks as you like. They will spawn in the center and can be dragged around the plate. Right click to remove them. Once you are ready, click the "Plate Bacteria" button to plate the bacteria. The simulation must be reset before antibiotics can be added/removed again.

Just like the real lab, drag the ruler to measure the zone of inhibition around each antibiotic disk. The zone of inhibition is the area around the disk where bacteria are unable to grow due to the concentration of the antibiotic.

3. What source was used for the susceptibility table and the diffusion rates?

The data for the susceptibility table has been adapted from the Clinical and Laboratory Standards Institute (CLSI) M100-Ed30™. The diffusion rates come from the accepted ranges defined by the CLSI.

CLSI. Performance Standards for Antimicrobial Susceptibility Testing. 30th ed. CLSI supplement M100. Wayne, PA: Clinical and Laboratory Standards Institute; 2020.
